Main Dishes

Ramen

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 pack instant ramen noodles (discard the seasoning or use half)
  • 1 clove garlic, minced (optional)
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sesame oil (optional)
  • 1 soft-boiled or fried egg
  • Sliced green onions
  • Optional toppings: mushrooms, spinach, corn, or cooked meat

Instructions:

  1. Simmer broth with garlic and soy sauce.
  2. Add noodles and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  3. Stir in sesame oil, then pour into a bowl.
  4. Top with egg, green onions, and extras.
  5. Slurp and enjoy!

Ramen

Ramen

A comforting Japanese noodle soup with springy noodles in rich broth.

Sushi

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup sushi rice (cooked & seasoned with vinegar, sugar, salt)
  • 1 sheet nori (seaweed)
  • 1/4 cucumber, julienned
  • 1/2 avocado, sliced
  • 50g fresh tuna or salmon (or crab sticks)
  • Soy sauce and wasabi (to serve)

Steps:

  1. Spread rice evenly on nori, leaving a 1-inch border at the top.
  2. Arrange fish, avocado, and cucumber in a line near the bottom.
  3. Roll tightly using a bamboo mat, wetting the nori edge to seal.
  4. Slice into 6-8 pieces with a wet knife.
  5. Serve with soy sauce and wasabi!

Sushi

Sushi

A Japanese dish featuring vinegared rice combined with fresh fish, vegetables, or other ingredients, often wrapped in seaweed (nori).

Tempura

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup ice-cold water
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • Assorted vegetables (sweet potato, zucchini, eggplant)
  • Shrimp or other seafood (optional)
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Tentsuyu dipping sauce (or tempura sauce)

Steps:

  1. Whisk egg and ice water in a bowl. Sift in flour and cornstarch, mix lightly (leave lumps).
  2. Heat oil to 340-350°F (170-180°C).
  3. Dip vegetables/seafood in batter, fry until golden (2-3 minutes).
  4. Drain on a wire rack or paper towels.
  5. Serve immediately with dipping sauce!

Tempura

Tempura

A Japanese dish of deep-fried seafood and vegetables in a light, crispy batter, served with a savory dipping sauce.

Tonkatsu

Ingredients:

  • 2 pork loin chops (1-inch thick)
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • 1 cup panko breadcrumbs
  • Vegetable oil for frying
  • Tonkatsu sauce (or Worcestershire sauce)
  • Shredded cabbage (for serving)

Steps:

  1. Season pork with salt and pepper. Dredge in flour, dip in egg, then coat with panko.
  2. Heat oil to 340°F (170°C). Fry pork for 5-6 minutes until golden brown.
  3. Drain on a wire rack and rest for 3 minutes.
  4. Slice into strips and serve with cabbage and tonkatsu sauce.

Tonkatsu

Tonkatsu

A Japanese crispy breaded pork cutlet, deep-fried to golden perfection and served with tangy tonkatsu sauce.

Yakitori

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb chicken thighs (boneless, skin-on)
  • 3-4 green onions (negi)
  • Bamboo skewers (soaked in water)
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp mirin
  • 1 tbsp sake
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • Salt (for shio-yakitori option)

Steps:

  1. Cut chicken into 1-inch cubes and green onions into 1-inch pieces.
  2. Skewer chicken alternately with green onions (3-4 pieces per skewer).
  3. For tare sauce: Simmer soy sauce, mirin, sake and sugar until slightly thickened.
  4. Grill skewers over medium-high heat, turning frequently. Brush with tare sauce in last 2 minutes.
  5. Serve immediately (optionally with shichimi togarashi).

Yakitori

Yakitori

Japanese grilled chicken skewers, typically seasoned with either savory tare sauce or salt, featuring juicy thigh meat and crisp green onions.

Donburi

Ingredients (Gyudon Style):

  • 1/2 lb thinly sliced beef (ribeye or chuck)
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 cups cooked Japanese short-grain rice
  • 1/2 cup dashi stock
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp mirin
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • Pickled red ginger (beni shoga) for garnish

Steps:

  1. In a pan, combine dashi, soy sauce, mirin and sugar. Bring to simmer.
  2. Add onions and cook for 3 minutes until translucent.
  3. Add beef slices and cook for 2-3 minutes until just cooked through.
  4. Spoon beef and sauce over bowls of hot rice.
  5. Top with grated ginger and beni shoga. Optional egg yolk in center.

Donburi

Donburi

Japanese rice bowl dish featuring simmered meat (typically beef or chicken) and vegetables served over steamed rice, often topped with egg.


Side Dishes

Miso Soup

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups dashi stock
  • 3-4 tbsp miso paste (white or red)
  • 1/2 block silken tofu, cubed
  • 1/4 cup wakame seaweed (rehydrated)
  • 2 green onions, thinly sliced

Steps:

  1. Heat dashi stock until just simmering (not boiling).
  2. Dissolve miso paste in a ladle with some hot broth, then return to pot.
  3. Add tofu and wakame, simmer for 2 minutes.
  4. Garnish with green onions and serve immediately.

Miso Soup

Miso Soup

Traditional Japanese soup made with dashi stock and fermented miso paste, containing tofu and seaweed.

Sunomono

Ingredients:

  • 1 English cucumber, thinly sliced
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1/2 tsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds
  • Optional: wakame seaweed or crab sticks

Steps:

  1. Toss cucumber slices with salt, let sit for 10 minutes then squeeze out excess water.
  2. Mix vinegar, sugar and soy sauce until sugar dissolves.
  3. Combine dressing with cucumbers and optional ingredients.
  4. Chill for 30 minutes, garnish with sesame seeds before serving.

Sunomono

Sunomono

Refreshing Japanese cucumber salad lightly pickled in sweet vinegar dressing, often with seafood or seaweed.

Edamame

Ingredients:

  • 12 oz fresh or frozen edamame in pods
  • 1 tbsp sea salt
  • Optional: 1/2 tsp chili flakes
  • Optional: 1 tsp sesame oil

Steps:

  1. Bring 4 cups water to boil, add 2 tsp salt.
  2. Add edamame, boil for 3-5 minutes until bright green.
  3. Drain and toss with remaining salt and optional seasonings.
  4. Serve warm or chilled. Squeeze pods to pop beans out.

Edamame

Edamame

Young soybeans in pods, lightly boiled and salted - a protein-rich Japanese appetizer enjoyed by popping beans from pods.

Tamagoyaki

Ingredients:

  • 4 large eggs
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp mirin
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tbsp vegetable oil

Steps:

  1. Whisk eggs with sugar, soy sauce, mirin and salt until smooth.
  2. Heat oil in rectangular tamagoyaki pan over medium-low.
  3. Pour thin layer of egg, let set slightly then roll toward one side.
  4. Repeat process, building layers until all egg is used.
  5. Slice into 1-inch pieces when slightly cooled.

Tamagoyaki

Tamagoyaki

Japanese rolled omelette with sweet-savory flavor, made by cooking thin egg layers in a rectangular pan.

Gomaae

Ingredients (Spinach Version):

  • 1 bunch spinach (about 10oz)
  • 3 tbsp toasted sesame seeds
  • 1 1/2 tbsp sugar
  • 1 1/2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp mirin

Steps:

  1. Blanch spinach for 30 seconds, rinse in cold water and squeeze dry.
  2. Grind sesame seeds in suribachi or mortar until slightly oily.
  3. Mix in sugar, soy sauce and mirin to make paste.
  4. Toss spinach with sesame mixture until well coated.
  5. Serve at room temperature or chilled.

Gomaae

Gomaae

Japanese sesame-dressed vegetables, most commonly spinach, featuring a nutty-sweet sesame seed coating.

Tsukemono

Ingredients (Quick Cucumber Pickle):

  • 2 Japanese cucumbers
  • 1 tbsp salt
  • 2 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp grated ginger
  • Optional: 1 tsp sesame seeds or chili threads

Steps:

  1. Cut cucumbers into 1/4-inch rounds or spears.
  2. Toss with salt and let sit for 15 minutes, then rinse.
  3. Mix vinegar, sugar and ginger until sugar dissolves.
  4. Combine with cucumbers and optional ingredients.
  5. Refrigerate for at least 1 hour before serving.

Tsukemono

Tsukemono

Japanese pickled vegetables that accompany meals, ranging from lightly salted to fermented varieties.
